3/20/30/40 RULE FOR HOME BUYER

 

New RBI mandate on home loan: What has changed?

But in a notification released on August 18,2023, the RBI asked lenders to provide borrowers the options to either increase the EMI or extend the loan tenure, or use both options together at the time of resetting interest rates on home loans. Sharma explains the RBI’s new new mandate in four points:

  • Lenders should communicate to the borrowers the possible impact of a change in benchmark rates leading to a change in EMI.
  • At the time of interest reset, borrowers should be given an option of switching over to a fixed rate of interest. All applicable charges for switching from floating to fixed should be disclosed in the loan sanction letter.
  • Borrowers should be given an option of extending loan tenures or enhancement in EMIs or both.

Example:

We can visualize this better with Priya’s example. Priya, lives in Pune, works for an IT company and has a in-hand salary of 15 Lakhs per annum.

By the 3/20/30/40 Rule, she should

1. Buy a house which is priced at a maximum of 3 times her annual income of 15 Lakh which is 45 Lakhs.

2. She should go for a 20 year loan tenure for the bank that funds the property purchase.

3. The sum total of all EMIs like education loan EMI, home loan EMI, personal loan EMIs should be less than 40% of monthly income which is 1,25,000.Which brings the allowed amount to: 37,500.

4. The down payment on property purchase should be 40%.18 Lakhs should be the down payment on the property costing 45 Lakhs

3: Value of house should not exceed 3 times of your annual income.

20: Tenure of loan should not be more than 20 years.

30: All EMI’s combined of house should not be more than 30% of your monthly income

40: Pay 40% of house cost as down payment.
